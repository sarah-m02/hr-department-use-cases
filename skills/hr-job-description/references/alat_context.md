# Alat Glossary — Strict, Sourced

**This file is a GLOSSARY, not a description.** Every term below has been
verified against a page on `alat.com` (or a credible secondary source
naming Alat's own public copy). Terms not verified against a public Alat
source are **not in this file**.

The `hr-job-description` skill treats this glossary as literal:

- If an Alat-specific term is needed and it is not in this glossary → do
  NOT invent one; ask the user via the AskUserQuestion "Other" free-text
  fallback and use whatever the user types verbatim.
- Every entry is annotated with a source.
- Verbatim wording matters — do not rephrase capitalized names or program
  titles, even slightly.

**Company name:** written as **Alat** (capital A, lowercase rest) — one word,
not the acronym "ALAT" or "ALat." That is the company's own styling.

Verification date: 2026-07 (via `alat.com/en/`, `alat.com/en/careers/`,
Wikipedia, Forbes Middle East, Arab News).

## 9. Hard-content prohibitions

The JD MUST NOT contain:

- Any specific compensation figure, range, bonus structure, allowance
  amount, or benefit dollar value
- Any Vision 2030 target percentage or year, or the phrase *"Vision 2030"*
- The name of any Saudi national programme (Saudization, Nitaqat,
  Tamheer, HRDF, etc.)
- **Any mention of PIF, "Public Investment Fund," or Alat's ownership by PIF**
- Named individuals (Chairman, CEO, Board members)
- Any specific headcount figure or the *"39,000 jobs"* / *"$9.3 billion"* targets
- Any mention of Arabic language as required, preferred, or optional
- Any *"Recommended"* label on an MCQ option
- Any invented business unit, sub-entity, or team name (see §10)
- Any of the historical business units that Alat no longer publicly lists
  (semiconductors, smart appliances as a separate unit, next-generation
  infrastructure as a separate unit)

## 10. Smart-Division MCQ logic (for the skill)

Given a role title, the skill's Question E options are built from this
glossary. Four options are picked in this order of preference and shown
without any *"Recommended"* tag:

**A. Role title cues a business unit:**

| Trigger keyword | Options in order |
|---|---|
| "AI," "digital," "compute," "hardware," "chip," "smart device," "data centre" | AI & Digital Hardware · Alat–Lenovo · Alat AIVisio · Other |
| "construction," "building," "heavy," "infrastructure," "industrial equipment" | Building & Heavy Equipment · Electrification · AI & Digital Hardware · Other |
| "energy," "power," "grid," "electric," "renewable," "generation," "transmission" | Electrification · Building & Heavy Equipment · AI & Digital Hardware · Other |
| "medical," "health," "consumer," "appliance," "home" | Home & Medical Equipment · Electrification · AI & Digital Hardware · Other |

**B. Role title cues a horizontal / corporate function** (Legal, Risk, Compliance, Audit, Finance, HR / People, Comms, Legal, IT, Strategy, ESG, Procurement, etc.):

Options: `Corporate function or specialist team (please specify) · AI & Digital Hardware · Building & Heavy Equipment · Other`

When the user picks the first option, use the AskUserQuestion `Other`
free-text fallback to capture the team name **verbatim** and use exactly
that string in the JD's Work Details Block and Role Overview. Do NOT map
it to a guessed department name.

**C. Fallback (no keyword rule fires):**

`AI & Digital Hardware · Building & Heavy Equipment · Electrification · Other`

## 11. Hard rules the skill MUST obey when using this glossary

1. Never emit any of the items on the §9 forbidden list.
2. Never invent a business unit, sub-entity, or team name beyond §3, §4, §5.
3. Never reference historical Alat structures (7-unit taxonomy, semiconductors,
   next-generation infrastructure as a distinct unit) in a 2026+ JD.
4. Never mention PIF or Alat's ownership by PIF in any artifact.
5. Never use *"Recommended"* as an MCQ label.
6. Never assert Arabic as a required, preferred, or optional language.
7. Never name individuals (Chairman, CEO, Board) in the JD body.
8. Never quote a specific headcount figure or the 39,000-jobs / $9.3B target.
9. Verbatim business-unit names in §3 must be preserved exactly — no
   reordering, renaming, or shortening.
10. Every Alat-specific claim in a JD should trace to an entry in this
    glossary. If it doesn't, question it — or ask the user to specify.
